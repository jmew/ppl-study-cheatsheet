Weather Reports & Flight Information

METAR & TAF

METAR

A current observation of conditions at an airport, issued hourly.

TAF

A forecast for the area within ~5 SM of an airport. TAF visibility only goes up to 6 SM — shown as P6SM ("greater than 6 SM").

Decoded example

KCGS 211853Z 22015G25KT 10SM FEW050 SCT250 27/14 A2992
KCGS
Station identifier (College Park).
211853Z
21st of the month, 1853 Zulu (UTC).
22015G25KT
Wind from 220° at 15 kts, gusting 25.
10SM
Visibility 10 statute miles.
FEW050
SCT250
Few clouds at 5,000′
scattered at 25,000′ (heights ×100 ft).
27/14
Temperature 27°C / dew point 14°C. (An M means minus: M07 = −7°C.)
A2992
Altimeter setting 29.92 "Hg.

Sky cover

Coverage is reported in eighths (oktas) of the sky. A ceiling is the lowest broken or overcast layer.

Winds & temps aloft

Each group reads direction + speed + temperature. The trick: if the coded direction is over 36, the wind is over 100 kts — subtract 50 from the direction and add 100 to the speed.

1 · High winds (direction over 36)

Temp −49°C. Above 24,000 ft it's always negative, so the sign is dropped.

Temp +3°C. Below 24,000 ft the temperature carries a + or − sign.

AIRMETs, SIGMETs & briefings

AIRMET (light aircraft)

  • Sierra — IFR / mountain obscuration
  • Tango — Turbulence
  • Zulu — Icing

SIGMET (severe, all aircraft)

  • Severe turbulence / icing
  • Widespread dust, sand, volcanic ash
  • Convective SIGMET = thunderstorms

Weather briefings

Standard
A full pre-flight briefing.
Abbreviated
An update to a briefing you already received.
Outlook
For a departure 6 or more hours away.
Frequency

Flight Service is on 122.2.

When you call, give…

Identify yourself up front: that you're a pilot, VFR or IFR, and your aircraft ID (N-number) — then your route, altitude, and timing.

Prognostic charts

Significant weather prog charts

A significant weather prognostic ("prog") chart forecasts hazards — IFR/MVFR areas, turbulence, and freezing levels — split into altitude blocks (panels), so you can see which areas and altitudes to avoid. (The interactive GFA is the modern replacement.)
