Testing Supplement Cheatsheet

During the PAR knowledge test the FAA hands you the Airman Knowledge Testing Supplement — a booklet of figures and legends. Everything below is printed in that booklet, so you can look it up instead of memorizing it. Each row links to the matching spot on this site.

How to use this page

Biggest look-up wins

Nearly every computational item on Performance & W&B, Weather Reports, and Navigation is a supplied graph, table, or chart. Focus your memorizing on Regulations and Human Factors — those are almost entirely recall.

Figure/legend numbers below are from FAA-CT-8080-2H (2018). They're stable but can shift between editions — confirm your testing center uses the same one.

Standing conventions (noted on the legends)

On charts & the Chart Supplement: bearings/radials are magnetic, times are UTC (Zulu), and elevations are MSL.

Not in the supplement — memorize these

There's no figure for the following, so they're pure recall:

Rules & numbers

  • Almost all of Regulations — ARROW, required equipment, right-of-way, cruising altitudes, speed limits, fuel reserves, inspections, currency, medicals, alcohol, NTSB timing, endorsements
  • Airspace numbers: VFR minimums & cloud clearances (152/111), Mode C veil
  • ATC: light-gun signals, squawk codes
  • Most FAR/AIM items

Concepts

  • Aerodynamics theory: four forces, drag, left-turning tendencies, stability, stalls/spins, ground effect
  • Systems: pitot-static, engine/carb ice/magneto, altitude & airspeed types
  • Weather concepts: pressure, fronts, thunderstorms, fog, icing
  • All of Human Factors
