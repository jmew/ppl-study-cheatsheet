Navigation Planning

Flight-computer fundamentals: applying magnetic variation and the speed–distance–time relationship.

Magnetic variation

Variation is the angle between true north and magnetic north at your location. Lines of equal variation on a chart are isogonic lines (the line of zero variation is the agonic line).

"East is least, West is best"

Converting a true heading to a magnetic heading:

  • W — West variation → add it to the heading ("best" = bigger)
  • E — East variation → subtract it from the heading ("least" = smaller)
Example

True heading 090° with 10° W variation → magnetic heading 100° (add). With 10° E variation → 080° (subtract).

Units: the knot

1 knot = 1 nautical mile per hour, so keep time in hours in the formulas (30 min = 0.5 hr).

Example

At a groundspeed of 120 kts, a 60 NM leg takes T = 60 ÷ 120 = 0.5 hr (30 minutes).
